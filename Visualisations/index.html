Recover from the pandemic in the great outdoors

COVID19 and the Outdoors

The outdoors has always been a big part of how people enjoy Australia, whether it be locals or tourists. In the line graph immediately below showing the number of people visiting tourist activities, we can see that our main tourist attractions are the outdoors, and before the pandemic they were seeing more and more visitors each year. The graph on the left shows the number of domestic holidays in a region by capita. Using the filters at the bottom, you can see how the pandemic has affected domestic travel.

Unfortunately, recent lockdowns and travel restrictions have impacted our interest in camping. We can see from the line graph below that restrictions led to an increase in indoor entertainment, to the neglect of time in the great outdoors.

Weather

So, now that restrictions are finally easing why not spend sometime outdoors with friends?

The first step to planning a trip is to decide your location. There’s a diverse range of places in our lucky state of Victoria, with unique weather too. The graphs below show the rainfall in areas across the state. Enjoy the rain? Head out to North East Victoria in August. Otherwise, if you’re looking to escape the wet, Mallee in the North West of Victoria is the place to be!
